#    Phishing Cybercrime Case Study

Phishing is a type of cybercrime in which attackers try to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank information , or OTPs by pretending to be a trusted source. This usually happens through emails, messages, or fake websites that look real and trusted. 

For example, a college student may recieve an email that appears to be from a bank and asking them to update their account details urgently. The email contains a link that redirects him to a fake website which is designed as such that it looks like the official bank webiste. When the student will enter his login credentials , the attacker will gain acces to his account.

Phishing attacks are commonly targeted for students and working professionals who may not verify the authenticity of such messages. The consequenes can include financial loss, identity theft, and unauthorised acces to personal accounts.

This type of cybercrime is on the rise as the use of cyber is on rise.It is due to the widesppread use of digital platforms. It is important for users to stay alert and avoid clicking on suspicious or unknown links or sharing confidential information online.
